    • Simple 10-Step Recipe for Banana Bread

    a quick and easy way to make a scrumptious loaf of banana bread, curated by a banana bread enthusiast

    overripe bananas work best when making banana bread since they are softer and easier to mash. Also, they are sweeter since as they age, the bananas' starches convert into sugars!

    If you have some overripe bananas sitting around, this recipe is perfect for turning them into a warm loaf of banana bread that can be served deliciously at any time of the day.
    Whether you're an experienced baker or a novice to the kitchen, this recipe is super simple and can be executed by anyone! There are many varients to making banana bread.
    Some people love adding oats, nuts, chocolate chips or other spices such as cinnamon or nutmeg to make the process fun and to add some texture to the bread.
    let's get started!

    this recipe will take about an hour and 15 minutes to complete (bake time: 1hr) then you can serve and enjoy!

    Ingredients

    • 3 Overrripe Bananas
    • 1 Cup Sugar
    • 1 Egg
    • 1/2 Stick Butter (melted)
    • 1 1/2 Cups All purpose flour
    • 1 Teaspoon Baking Soda
    • 1 Teaspoon Salt
    • Optional Items
      • Walnuts
      • Chocolate Chips
      • Vanilla extract or Maple Syrup
      • Anything else you can think of!

    Instructions

    1. Gather all ingredients and preheat oven to 325 degrees Fahrenheit
    2. Greese a 9x5-inch loaf pan
    3. Mash the bananas inside a large bowl
    4. Add in the rest of the wet ingredients into the bowl, such as the eggs, melted butter, vanilla extract (if added), etc
    5. In another bowl, mix the dry ingredients together: flour, baking soda, salt, sugar, nuts, chocolate chips, etc
    6. Once both bowls are mixed, conjoin the dry and wet mixtures until the batter is mixed evenly throughout
    7. Pour the combined batter into the greesed baking platter
    8. Bake in the oven for about an hour-- the loaf should be golden crusted and fully baked inside.
      To check whether it is ready, you can use a toothpick and insert it through the loaf. If the toothpick comes out clean, the bread is ready!
    9. Take out the bread and let it sit for about 10-15 minutes to cool down and settle
    10. Serve and enjoy your creation!
    I personally love banana bread with coffee, the flavors and textures just match so well together!
